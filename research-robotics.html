Bio-inspired Robotics

Developing robots inspired by marine organisms

RoboKrill: Shrimp-Inspired Robotic Appendage

The Pleobot is a modular robotic platform that replicates the swimming kinematics of krill and shrimp. This shrimp-like appendage accurately reproduces the four main kinematic angles that characterize metachronal propulsion: α (alpha), β (beta), γ (gamma), and ζ (zeta). These angles control the motion of the protopodite, the biramous distal appendage, the abduction/adduction of the exopodite and endopodite, and the cupping angle, respectively.

Leading-Edge Vortex

Using Particle Image Velocimetry (PIV), we revealed for the first time the formation of a leading-edge vortex (LEV) on the exopodite during the power stroke. This LEV contributes to lift generation through a suction effect, representing a novel discovery in metachronal swimming mechanics. The attached LEV remains stable at low angles of attack and enhances hydrodynamic performance.
